1. ABSTRACT
================================================================================

  S-MAS is a high-fidelity satellite operational digital twin that uses
  Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL) to autonomously optimize the
  orbital lifetime of a Low Earth Orbit (LEO) microsatellite. The system
  models the ESA PROBA-1 spacecraft (NORAD 26957) at ~600 km altitude,
  integrating real historical space weather data (2001-2020), radiation
  hazard maps, and a validated atmospheric drag model.

  The platform spans four programming languages across a six-phase pipeline:
  C++ (physics engine), Python (MARL training), C# (operational controller),
  and TypeScript/React (WebGPU 3D dashboard). Three heterogeneous AI agents
  (Navigation, Bus Management, Mission) coordinate under a rule-based FDIR
  safety governor using the MAPPO algorithm with Centralized Training and
  Decentralized Execution (CTDE).

  The physics engine was validated against 30 days of real PROBA-1 TLE data
  using SGP4 propagation, achieving an MSE of 336.5 km^2 after calibration.

  Post-deployment analysis identified three critical failure modes — battery
  Arrhenius over-aging, panel drift mismatch, and eclipse drain — which were
  resolved through targeted engine and governor updates, extending operational
  lifetime from 442 days to well beyond 600+ days (ongoing).

4. PHASE 1: C++ PHYSICS ENGINE
================================================================================

  4.1 Orbital Mechanics

    - 4th-order Runge-Kutta (RK4) integrator at dt = 5.0 s
    - Earth gravity: point-mass + J2 oblateness perturbation
    - Aerodynamic drag: F_D = -0.5 * rho * Cd * A * v^2 * v_hat
    - Atmosphere co-rotation correction: v_rel = v - (omega x r)
    - Thrust: continuous 3D vector with throttle [0, 1]
    - 5% throttle deadband applied to prevent micro-thrust jitter

  4.2 NRLMSISE-00 Atmospheric Model (Simplified)

    - Exospheric temperature: T_inf = f(F10.7, F10.7_avg, Ap)
    - Bates-Walker temperature profile: T(h) = T_inf - (T_inf - T_120) * exp(-sigma * dh)
    - Diffusive equilibrium density with diurnal and latitudinal modulation
    - density_multiplier = 0.01 (calibrated for multi-year orbital lifetime)
      Short-term validation (30 days): 0.1 gives MSE=336.5 km^2
      Long-term realism (multi-year): 0.01 for realistic ~20+ yr decay at 600km

  4.3 Subsystems Modeled

    - SatelliteBus: GaAs solar arrays (~90 W), Li-ion battery with Arrhenius
      degradation, eclipse/sunlight power cycling
    - Stochastic layer: sensor noise, actuator delay (1-3 steps), actuator
      error (+/-5%), epistemic Cd drift (random walk)
    - SEU generator: probabilistic single-event upsets during SAA transit
    - Ground station visibility: spherical LoS with 5-degree elevation mask

  4.4 Degradation Model (Calibrated)

    Battery Arrhenius Model:
      loss_per_cycle = BATT_CYCLE_DEGRAD * (1 + BATT_THERMAL_FACT * cycles)
      capacity      *= (1 - loss_per_cycle)

      Parameter           | Original  | Calibrated | Effect
      --------------------|-----------|------------|--------------------------------
      BATT_CYCLE_DEGRAD   | 0.00005   | 0.00002    | 2.5x slower base degradation
      BATT_THERMAL_FACT   | 0.001     | 0.00005    | 20x slower acceleration factor
      Battery EOL (80%)   | ~Year 1.7 | ~Year 14   | Matches real satellite data

    Panel Degradation:
      Parameter           | Original  | Calibrated | Effect
      --------------------|-----------|------------|--------------------------------
      PANEL_DRIFT_SIGMA   | 0.0001/step | 0.00003/step | 3.3x slower random walk
      Secular loss rate   | 1e-8/step | 2e-9/step  | 5x slower (1.5%/yr realistic)
      Floor               | 0.40      | 0.30       | Matches C++ engine clamp

  4.5 C API (DLL Export)

5. PHASE 2: CORE MARL FRAMEWORK
================================================================================

  5.1 Algorithm: MAPPO (Multi-Agent PPO)

    - Shared policy architecture (all agents share weights)
    - Centralized Training, Decentralized Execution (CTDE)
    - GAE (lambda = 0.95, gamma = 0.99) for advantage estimation
    - Clip ratio: 0.2, entropy coefficient: 0.01
    - 16 parallel environments during training (num_envs = 16)
    - Episode length: 120,960 steps (~1 week simulated time)

  5.2 Observation Space (29 dimensions)

    Category       | Features                              | Dims
    ---------------|---------------------------------------|-----
    Orbital        | alt, lat, lon, |v|, vx_norm, vy_norm  | 6
    Power          | SoC, capacity_frac, solar_w, draw_w   | 4
    Environment    | rho_log, flux10_log, flux30_log, ecl, saa | 5
    Communication  | gs_visible, time_since_contact_norm   | 2
    FDIR           | one-hot [NOM, DEG, SAFE, REC]         | 4
    Degradation    | panel_eff, cd_norm, cycles_norm       | 3
    SEU            | seu_active                            | 1
    Lag features   | kp_3h, f107_3h, kp_6h, f107_6h       | 4

  5.3 Agent Action Spaces

    Agent       | Action Type | Space
    ------------|-------------|-------------------------------
    Navigation  | Continuous  | thrust_xyz [-1,1]^3 + throttle [0,1]
    Bus Manager | Discrete    | deep_sleep {0, 1}
    Mission     | Discrete    | payload_on {0, 1}

  5.4 Reward Function

    R_t = w_alive - w_fuel * dV_proxy - w_dod * DoD
        - w_alt * |h - 600| - w_fatal * P_fatal

    Key weights (Phase 3, calibrated):
      w_fatal = 50,000  (100x increase from Phase 2 to prevent reward hacking)
      w_dod   = tuned to penalize deep battery discharge
      Mission: +50.0 valid target, -500.0 SAA imaging

  5.5 Progressive Degradation Training

    At each episode reset, the engine starts at a random mission age:
      capacity_j    ~ Uniform(120,000 J, 360,000 J)   [prev: 200k-360k]
      panel_eff     ~ Uniform(0.45, 1.0)               [prev: 0.70-1.0]

    During the episode, hardware degrades progressively (orbit-by-orbit):
      panel_decay   = 0.0017 per orbit  (→ ~18% drop over 1 week)
      capacity_decay = 645 J per orbit  (→ ~67 kJ drop over 1 week)
      Floor: panel_eff >= 0.30, capacity_j >= 80,000 J

6. PHASE 3: MISSION LAYER
================================================================================

  6.1 Mission Agent (CHRIS Instrument Control)

    - Toggles optical payload ON/OFF based on orbital position
    - Power draw penalty when active (accelerates discharge)
    - +50.0 reward for valid target imaging (one-shot per pass)
    - -500.0 penalty for payload ON inside SAA boundaries
    - Greed prevention: imaging below 30% SoC yields net negative reward

  6.2 Meta-Coordination

    - If Bus agent activates deep_sleep, payload is forced OFF
    - FDIR governor overrides all agents during DEGRADED/SAFE modes
    - Eclipse conservation: deep sleep forced during ALL eclipse passages

  6.3 FDIR State Machine (C++ Engine)

    State      | SoC Trigger | AI Control
    -----------|-------------|----------------------------------
    NOMINAL    | > 20%       | Full autonomy
    DEGRADED   | < 20%       | Payload OFF, thrust capped at 30%
    SAFE       | < 10%       | All overridden, deep sleep forced
    RECOVERY   | > 15%       | Payload OFF, transitioning back

  6.4 C# Power Safety Governor (Multi-Layer)

    The C# governor provides an independent safety layer on top of the
    C++ engine's FDIR. It runs every simulation step before sending
    actions to the engine:

    Layer                | Trigger         | Action
    ---------------------|-----------------|--------------------------------
    Critical Power       | SoC < 30%       | Force deep sleep + payload off
    Warning Power        | SoC < 60%       | Force payload off
    Eclipse Conservation | in_eclipse == 1 | Force deep sleep + payload off
    FDIR Passthrough     | SAFE mode       | Full override (deep sleep)

    Eclipse Conservation is the primary defense against late-life battery
    death. During eclipse (zero solar input), deep sleep reduces bus power
    from 30W to 5W, cutting eclipse energy consumption by 83%:
      Without: 30W x 2100s = 63,000 J per eclipse (22% of degraded battery)
      With:     5W x 2100s = 10,500 J per eclipse ( 3.7% of degraded battery)

8. PHASE 5: WEBGPU DASHBOARD
================================================================================

  8.1 Technology: Vite + React 19 + TypeScript

  8.2 Rendering Engine

    - Primary: WebGPU with WGSL shaders (instanced rendering)
    - Fallback: WebGL2 with GLSL shaders (automatic detection)
    - Wireframe Earth (bright cyan-blue) + FDIR-colored satellite dot
    - Coordinate transform: Lat/Lon/Alt -> ECEF (X, Y, Z)

  8.3 Dynamic SAA Heatmap

    Previous: Heatmap sampled from static UV coordinates, appearing as a
    "sticker" fixed to the sphere mesh regardless of Earth rotation.

    Fixed: Both WebGPU and WebGL shaders now compute geographic UV
    coordinates from the vertex's local position and the Earth rotation
    angle uniform, so the heatmap remains geo-referenced:
      1. Vertex shader passes local position (vLocalPos) to fragment shader
      2. Fragment shader undoes Earth rotation: geoPos = rotateY(-angle) * localPos
      3. Computes geoLat/geoLon from geoPos
      4. Maps to UV: geoUV = ( (lon+pi)/(2*pi), 1-(lat+pi/2)/pi )
      5. Samples heatmap at geoUV — SAA stays over South Atlantic

  8.4 Telemetry Decoder (useTelemetry hook)

    - Zero-allocation DataView parser
    - Directly decodes binary WebSocket stream to React state
    - No JSON parsing overhead
    - Auto-reconnect WebSocket: retries every 2 seconds

  8.5 HUD Design

    - Dynamic FDIR state indicators with pulse animations
    - Mission Lifetime counter: displays elapsed time as Xy Xd Xh format

    - Real-time SoC, altitude, agent actions, environment status
    - FDIR-colored satellite: blue (nominal), green (payload ON),

9. PHASE 6: VALIDATION WITH REAL TLE DATA
================================================================================

  9.1 Methodology

    - Parsed 43,000+ historical PROBA-1 TLEs from Space-Track
    - Used Python sgp4 library to establish ground-truth altitude curve
    - Ran S-MAS engine (zero thrust) for equivalent 30-day window
    - Compared simulated vs real decay profiles

  9.2 Density Multiplier Calibration

    Multiplier | MSE (km^2) | Use Case
    -----------|------------|-----------------------------
    0.01x      | N/A        | Long-term realism (20+ yr)
    0.10x      | 336.5      | Best 30-day accuracy
    0.25x      | 438.4      | -
    0.50x      | 619.3      | -
    0.75x      | 860.5      | -
    1.00x      | 1145.3     | Raw model (overestimates)

  9.3 Dual Calibration Strategy

    The simplified NRLMSISE-00 model overestimates thermospheric density
    by approximately 10-100x at 600 km. Two calibration points are used:

    - Short-term validation (30 days): multiplier = 0.1
      RMSE ~18.3 km, suitable for training and unit tests.

    - Long-term realism (multi-year): multiplier = 0.01
      Matches real PROBA-1 behavior (24-year operational life at ~600 km).
      Set as the default for operational simulations.

10. POST-DEPLOYMENT DEBUGGING & ENGINE HARDENING
================================================================================

  10.1 Root Cause Analysis — Battery Death Spiral

    The satellite consistently died at ~440 days (Phase 3 baseline), then
    ~616 days after initial fixes. Telemetry analysis revealed:

    Run 1 (442 days):
      Cause: Panel efficiency degraded to 58% (σ=0.0001/step was too fast)
      At 58% panels: solar output = 52.2W < payload + bus draw (55W)
      → AI could not sustain payload; battery drained orbit-by-orbit

    Run 2 (616 days):
      Cause: Battery Arrhenius degradation too aggressive
      After 9,262 cycles: remaining capacity = 7.4% (only 26 kJ)
      A single eclipse required 63 kJ → impossible to survive

    Run 3 (1y 270d / 635 days):
      Cause: Eclipse drain on degraded battery
      After 9,525 cycles, capacity ≈ 284 kJ (79% remaining — acceptable)
      But AI kept payload ON (55W) during sunlight, then entered eclipse
      with 0W solar and no automatic sleep mode
      Eclipse drain: 30W x 2100s = 63,000 J = 22% per eclipse
      Over 3-4 consecutive problem orbits → battery dead

  10.2 Fixes Applied

    Fix | File                          | Change
    ----|-------------------------------|------------------------------------------
    1   | constants.h                   | BATT_CYCLE_DEGRAD: 0.00005 → 0.00002
    2   | constants.h                   | BATT_THERMAL_FACT: 0.001 → 0.00005
    3   | constants.h                   | PANEL_DRIFT_SIGMA: 0.0001 → 0.00003
    4   | stochastic.cpp                | Secular panel loss: 1e-8 → 2e-9 per step
    5   | config.py                     | min_panel_eff: 0.40 → 0.30
    6   | config.py                     | min_capacity_j: 100,000 → 80,000 J
    7   | env_wrapper.py                | panel_eff start range: [0.70,1.0] → [0.45,1.0]
    8   | env_wrapper.py                | capacity start range: [200k,360k] → [120k,360k]
    9   | FdirGovernor.cs               | Payload-off threshold: 50% → 60% SoC
    10  | FdirGovernor.cs               | Eclipse conservation: force deep sleep always
    11  | train.py + InferenceEngine.cs | 5% throttle deadband (prevent micro-thrust)
    12  | reward.py                     | w_fatal: 500 → 50,000 (prevent death reward hack)
    13  | WebGLContext.ts               | Dynamic heatmap: geographic UV from earth rotation
    14  | WebGPUContext.ts              | Dynamic heatmap: same fix for WebGPU path

  10.3 Confirmed Not Bugs

    - RK4 integrator: correct 4th-order integration, J2 perturbation valid
    - Drag model: correct atmospheric co-rotation, physically sound
    - ABI alignment: StatePacket=184B, ActionPacket=19B — verified both sides
    - FDIR state machine: correct hysteresis thresholds for up/down transitions
    - Density multiplier: consistent 0.01 between Python training and C# ops

12. KNOWN LIMITATIONS
================================================================================

  1. Atmospheric model is a simplified NRLMSISE-00 approximation, not the
     full empirical model. Uses dual calibration (0.1 for short-term,
     0.01 for long-term realism).
  2. Training convergence requires substantial compute (>1M steps for
     meaningful policy improvement). Current agents exhibit orbit-raising
     behavior that reduces long-term stability.
  3. Single-satellite simulation. Constellation expansion (4 satellites)
     is planned — see PLAN.txt for full implementation roadmap.
  4. WebGPU support requires modern browsers (Chrome/Edge 113+).
  5. The C++ engine does not include SGP4 propagation internally; it uses
     Keplerian initialization + numerical RK4 integration.
  6. The modular algorithm design (GUIDE.md) allows swapping MAPPO for
     MADDPG, QMIX, IPPO, HAPPO, or other MARL algorithms by editing
     only 2 files (mappo.py + train.py imports).
  7. Missing subsystems for real deployment: thermal model, attitude
     dynamics (ADCS), propellant budget, higher-fidelity gravity model.
     Full roadmap documented in UPDATE.txt.
